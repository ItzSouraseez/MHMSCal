School Features


Mansur Habibullah Memorial School, formerly known as South End School was established in the year 1979 by Syed Abul Mansur Habibullah. He was the former speaker of West Bengal Legislative Assembly & Law Minister, Govt. of West Bengal. The school started with only 30 boys and girls. However, it made good progress over the years, and the strength has now become 2000 (approx). It obtained its affiliation from the Council for Indian School Certificate Examinations (C.I.S.C.E) in 1989. It is a Co-educational English medium school with classes from Pre-Nursery to class XII. The objective of the school is always to create a healthy environment for the students to facilitate real education. We strive to provide more focus to students to express themselves and cultivate their individual excellence. The school caters to the needs of the students and provides an all-round development of the students.


ASISC MEMBERSHIP CERTIFICATE.



Some of the unique features of the school are

  • Holistic Education with suitable facilities and amenities.
  • Spacious and attractive infrastructure with eco friendly classrooms.
  • Well stocked library with age appropriate books.
  • Well equipped, spacious and airy Science, Computer, Geography, Art and Home Science labs.
  • Caring, supportive and experienced teachers.
  • Excellent exposure and avenues in co-curricular activities.
  • Robust fire management system with 360 degree CCTV - Surveillance.

Incoming Exams

Exams are knocking at the door, Do you guys really want to play anymore? Lots of notes to study, lots of books to be read; Are these not enough for you to hold your head? Exams are knocking at the door, Don’t you guys want to prepare yourselves more? “There is no end to studying.”, and that, “Knowledge is like an ocean.”, they say. Think about these even though you believe You’ve studied enough and just want to go out to play. So dear friends, sisters and brothers, Please sit down to study; Unless you want to get scolded by your mothers, Don’t go out until, for the exams, you’re ready.

Shrayashi Maity, Class : XA

The Moon


The sun rises everyday with rays of hope

But for me it is the moon who has the rope

In the victory of enlightment;

To nullify all the adjustments ,

From making my mother smile,

Till making my father feel the pride.


I quietly sit near the window pane,

Thinking about how my life is in vain.

Then I see the gleaming moon,

Depending on the sun for her light ;

Still never losing her glint !

Her divine beauty , grace and admiration ,

Is beyond appreciation.

Debpriya Ghosh XII A

Being mostly happy but sometimes mellow.

Off to trips to ocean, hills and river,

camping and swimming, having fun;

Going away to the summer camp

And the glorious naps under the sun.
